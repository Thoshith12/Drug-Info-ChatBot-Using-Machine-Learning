            Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medications:

OTC medications are drugs that can be purchased directly by consumers without a prescription from a healthcare professional.
These drugs are typically used to treat minor health conditions such as headaches, colds, allergies, and indigestion.
They are considered safe for use without direct medical supervision, although it's important for individuals to carefully read and follow the instructions on the label.
Examples of OTC medications include aspirin, ibuprofen, cough syrups, antacids, allergy medications, and topical creams for minor skin irritations.

        
        Prescription Medications:

Prescription medications are drugs that can only be obtained with a written prescription from a licensed healthcare provider, such as a doctor or nurse practitioner.
These drugs are typically used to treat more serious health conditions or conditions that require a specific diagnosis and treatment plan.
Prescription medications are often more potent and may have more significant side effects compared to OTC medications.
They require supervision by a healthcare professional to ensure they are used safely and effectively.
Examples of prescription medications include antibiotics, antidepressants, blood pressure medications, and certain pain relievers.\
